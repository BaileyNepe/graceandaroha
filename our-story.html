Our story

Grace & Aroha came to be, after years of dreams and discussion between the two directors, Jay and Tamara

After working together in a Nursery in 2016, and realising our hopes and ideals for children, whānau and Kaiako were the same, we would regularly talk about these hopes and ideals. That every child deserves individualised care, structure and stability in their life, especially if (like many family situations today), they are unable to be cared for at home by whānau because of parents working or any other reason. We experienced that smaller ratios of children to Kaiako is hugely beneficial for children in the early years, and most importantly, that all people - children, whānau, Kaiako all thrive when cared for in respectful. loving environments.

Fast forward five years later, a few different childcare teaching positions, a couple more children, and a global pandemic, we knew now was the time to create our vision and work with whānau and teachers to provide the learning, support and care needed in early childhood care and education.

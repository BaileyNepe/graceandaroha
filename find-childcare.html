Childcare

We know it can be a big decision when finding the right care for your child. Grace & Aroha Educare believe that small group sizes benefit infants and young children. Children are able to form a close relationship with their kaiako which is integral to their health and wellbeing. The first 1000 days, in particular, of your child’s life is a time where relationships are everything. Our kaiako will work in partnership with you as they care and educate your child.

Values

We believe whānau should be supported with care as they raise their children. The three underlying principles of our kaupapa are love, care and respect. We will keep in touch regularly by sending photos, learning stories and quick updates. We want you to know that you are their number one person and that we will offer them a safe and caring space while you are away from them.

What we provide

Our kaiako provide a safe and homely environment for children to learn and grow. Small group ratios of one adult to a maximum of four children allows for meaningful relationships to develop and special moments of one to one care routines and daily rhythms and rituals to create a consistent and predictable environment. A variety of good quality resources are provided, as well as a stimulating and natural outdoor environment that will offer a space to foster physical challenges and time in nature. Grace & Aroha Educare believe that kaiako benefit from being surrounded by other like-minded kaiako. We encourage kaikao to meet up with one another where children play and learn alongside other small groups of children, get involved in community events and involve children in day to day life, learning about the world around them as well as caring for themselves and their environment.

What you need to provide

Each day please bring a bag with two to three changes of clothes, a hat (suitable for the season), sunscreen, nappies, wipes, lunchbox with morning tea, lunch and afternoon tea, formula, bottle, drink bottle,

What age child can be enrolled?

We are licenced to take children from the age of birth up to six years of age.

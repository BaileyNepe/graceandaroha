Our story

Grace & Aroha came to be, after years of dreams and discussion between two friends, Jay and Tamara.

After working together in a Nursery in 2016, and realising our hopes and ideals for children, whānau and Kaiako were the same, we would regularly talk about these hopes and ideals. This led to them becoming homebased providers. Although now, after 12 months, Tamara’s family have moved to Australia for further work opportunities and Jay has chosen to pursue her dream of providing a service to families offering a variety of support during the parenting journey. There is plenty of advice on getting pregnant, antenatal classes which predominantly focus on the birth, but what happens when you arrive home with this new precious being who relies solely on you!

Not everyone is comfortable to share how hard it can be and that it is okay to ask for help. Grace & Aroha come to serve you, lift you up, be a shoulder to lean on, and bring a caring and respectful view that sharing knowledge can empower another. There is no one answer to this journey, but there’s certainly nothing like having that champion on your side!

Parent Guide

Jay Fowles

Jay is a committed and passionate qualified early childhood teacher with a passion for supporting you to create a loving and caring home that encourages optimal learning for your baby. With years of experience working with infants and toddlers she is also passionate in supporting whānau through the challenging times at home and offers support, particularly with establishing positive sleep habits and positive behaviour strategies.
